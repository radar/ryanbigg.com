I've had a Comsol 3-in-1 wireless charger sitting on my desk for ages. It has a hardcore 2010s tech aesthetic, with its white plastic and chrome combination. I use it to charge my phone, watch and earbuds all at once. Or at least I did, until it stopped being able to charge the phone. It could still charge a watch and the earbuds, but I used it most for charging the phone.

The device still turned on, but it would flash a solid white light for about a second, then off again, then repeat. I didn't know what caused this, and googling for the manual turned up no results. But I did find the support email! So I contacted them and asked what it meant and if they had the manual.

They then asked me a few questions about how I've connected it, and I answered those. Then they replied with:

> Thanks for letting me know…
> I’m more than happy to replace the WMCS03 for you.
>
> Please supply your full name, an ATTENDED daytime address & mobile number.
> Our courier requires a signature upon delivery.
> They won’t leave it if no one is there. Typically a work address is fine.

At no point did I ask for a replacement, I was just wondering what the blinkenlight meant on the device. That they offered a replacement is something incredible in this era of customer service that typically resembles disassociative states.

That email exchange was Tuesday. The replacement device turned up today, 3 'days later, in a regional town. Even that is impressive!
